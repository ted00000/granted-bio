title: Stress Granule Formation in the Antiretroviral-Mediated Dysregulation of Oligodendrocyte Maturation in HIV-HAND
org_name: UNIVERSITY OF PENNSYLVANIA
phr: Project Narrative
Despite widespread use of antiretroviral (ARV) therapy, approximately half of people living with HIV exhibit
symptoms of HIV-Associated Neurocognitive disorder, a hallmark of which is the atrophy of white matter and the
loss of myelin. We have previously demonstrated that select ARV drugs inhibit maturation and differentiation of
oligodendrocytes, the cells responsible for myelination in the CNS. The proposed experiments aim to elucidate
the role that stress granules play in the inhibition of maturation in ARV treated oligodendrocytes, so as to better
understand the mechanisms of oligodendrocyte dysfunction as a prelude to designing therapies.
