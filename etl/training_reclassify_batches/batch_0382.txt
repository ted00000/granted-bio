phr: PROJECT NARRATIVE/LAY SUMMARY Stroke is one of the leading causes of disability in the United States with an annual incidence of 800,000, leaving many of these individuals with long-term motor deficits due to motor network damage, limiting their quality of life. This proposal aims to use stereoelectroencephalography based neurofeedback to simulate a damaged network and test the ability of the motor network to learn new motor patterns to overcome this damage. The results from this project will be impactful as they aim to uncover the fundamental nature of motor network adaptation and provide direct supporting evidence for stereoelectroencephalography neurofeedback as a new tool in post-stroke rehabilitation.
