Antisocial behavior is common in frontotemporal dementia and can have severe consequences when patients
break the law. The proposed research will use moral decision-making tests and neuroimaging to better
understand the cognitive and neural mechanisms leading to these behaviors. This knowledge will inform future
studies aimed at identifying, preventing, and treating antisocial behavior in dementia.

title: Elucidating Host Responses to AAV Ocular Gene Therapy Vectors
org_name: HARVARD MEDICAL SCHOOL
phr: Project Narrative Despite the clinical benefits of AAV ocular gene therapy vectors, inflammation and toxicity have been reported in some patients, at times resulting in visual deterioration. Prior literature and unpublished data suggest a role for dsRNA-mediated ocular toxicity of AAV vectors, which we will test directly in this proposal. Our data suggest that there are other pathways as well that contribute to toxicity, so we will leverage an unbiased spatial transcriptomics approach (Light-Seq) paired with functional readouts to identify and validate pathways mediating toxicity, expanding our understanding of antiviral responses to AAV in the eye.

title: Development and evaluation of neuroimaging biomarkers of structural and functional network degeneration in Alzheimer’s disease
org_name: UNIVERSITY OF TEXAS HLTH SCIENCE CENTER
phr: PROJECT NARRATIVE
Biomarkers play an expanding role in Alzheimer’s disease (AD) research; the amyloid, tau, neurodegeneration
(A/T/N) framework describes AD biomarkers by providing a binary (positive/negative) classification scheme for
each category. However, this framework ignores the anatomical distribution of pathology, which may provide
vital information on AD pathogenesis, symptomology, and treatment response. Here, we propose the
development of a spatially-based neuroimaging biomarker for AD, which will directly aid in monitoring disease
progression and has great potential to accelerate discovery of therapeutics targeted to underlying
neuropathology.
