Older adults with mild cognitive impairment experience twice as many falls as cognitively intact older adults; however, it has rarely been examined how mild cognitive impairment affects the ability to prevent falling and recover balance when unexpected perturbations are experienced. In this study, we will examine differences in reactive balance between older adults with and without mild cognitive impairment when exposed to trip perturbations while walking on a treadmill to investigate the mechanisms underlying increased fall risk in this population.

title: Biosynthesis of Several Oxyvinylglycine Nonproteinogenic Amino Acids Bearing Unusual Alkoxyamine Bonds
org_name: UNIV OF NORTH CAROLINA CHAPEL HILL
phr: PROJECT NARRATIVE
Natural products are an invaluable source of and inspiration for pharmaceuticals and agrochemicals. This
project proposes to study the biosynthetic pathways of bioactive nonproteinogenic amino acid secondary
metabolites that contain unusual vinyl N–O bonds. Understanding the synthesis of these small molecules and
the biochemical mechanisms underlying their formation will enable our search for similar bioactive compounds
and expand the toolbox of biocatalytic oxidative enzymes.
