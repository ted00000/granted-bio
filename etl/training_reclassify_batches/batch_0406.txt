phr: Project Narrative
The Initiative for Maximizing Student Development at the University at Buffalo facilitates successful completion of PhD degrees in biomedical and behavioral sciences. The goal is to provide trainees with research mentoring, career, and professional development to enhance outstanding research, excel in graduate school and successfully enter the biomedical and behavioral scientific workforce. A workforce of talented individuals bringing different perspectives will enhance team science research, discovery of novel therapies and reduce the impact of health disparities.

title: Targeting the MAP4K4 pathway to inhibit pediatric high-grade glioma invasion
org_name: STANFORD UNIVERSITY
phr: Project Narrative
High-grade gliomas (HGGs) pose a devastating prognosis for children, because they
are highly invasive tumors with minimal treatment options. The kinase MAP4K4 is an
identified negative prognostic indicator in several cancers; reducing its levels can
decrease tumor invasiveness and increase the effectiveness of chemotherapeutics. Our
research aims to improve and extend children’s lives, by focusing on this MAP4K4
pathway using cell lines and mouse models to design drugs targeting these tumors.
