title: Biologic and Functional Characterization of In Vivo CAR-T cells
org_name: STANFORD UNIVERSITY
phr: Project Narrative
Chimeric Antigen Receptor (CAR)-T cell immunotherapy has shown impressive response rates in B cell
malignancies; however, limitations to access and failure of many patients to reach complete response call for
therapeutic enhancement. Although the causes for CAR-T cell failure are multiple and still emerging, the time
necessary to manufacture CAR-T cells seems to directly correlate with quality: the longer the manufacturing
time, the worse performance of CAR-T cells once infused into the patient. We hypothesize that platforms
enabling in vivo engineering of CAR-T cells will enhance CAR-T cell quality and efficacy with less expense and
greater patient access.

title: Effects of locomotion on visual inter-areal processing
org_name: ALBERT EINSTEIN COLLEGE OF MEDICINE
phr: PROJECT NARRATIVE
By determining the role of locomotion in inter-areal communication, we seek to understand how communication
in the brain can be altered or improved by behavior. Better understanding of inter-areal signaling may provide
important insights into the neural substrates of disorders where signaling is altered, as in autism and
schizophrenia. Understanding changes in inter-areal communication with behavior may help us elucidate
mechanisms of existing therapies, such as mirror therapy, music therapy, and mindfulness, as well as develop
new therapies that help to restore optimal inter-areal communication.
