title: Internally and externally driven arousal changes in neural activity and hemodynamics
org_name: CARNEGIE-MELLON UNIVERSITY
phr: PROJECT NARRATIVE An individual’s level of engagement with their environment, often described as arousal, is impaired in a variety of neurologic disorders, including schizophrenia and Alzheimer’s. Systemic metrics of arousal, such as heart rate and pupil diameter, could be used as non-invasive, diagnostic markers if the link between arousal and brain activity was understood. The proposed research investigates how neural activity and cerebral hemodynamics slowly fluctuate with arousal level, measured systemically, and in turn influence behavior.
