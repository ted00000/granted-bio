title: Documenting the Home and Community Storytelling Practices of Mexican-American Bilingual Children with Developmental Language Disorder
org_name: UNIVERSITY OF CALIFORNIA-IRVINE
phr: Project Narrative
The goal of this study is to investigate the culturally-specific storytelling practices and styles of bilingual Latinx
children with and without Developmental Language Disorder (DLD). We will document the storytelling
practices and styles bilingual children with DLD participate in at home and in their communities. Lastly, we
will compare their performance on narratives using extant White, Educated, Industrialized, Rich, and
Democratic (WEIRD) measures with culturally-relevant measures that arise from the data, so that
speech-language pathologists may optimize assessment for this historically underserved population.

title: Mechanisms and Prognostic Value of Ictal Onset Patterns for Epilepsy Neurostimulation
org_name: NEW YORK UNIVERSITY SCHOOL OF MEDICINE
phr: PROJECT NARRATIVE
Responsive neurostimulation (RNS) is a brain implant for patients with drug-resistant epilepsy that delivers
electrical stimulation to the seizure onset zone. However, seizure reduction after RNS placement can vary
widely, and up to 25% of patients have little to no benefit. The goals of this study are to explore the underlying
mechanisms that give rise to ictal onset patterns (IOPs), determine their use in predicting outcomes after RNS
placement, and train artificial neural networks to classify IOPs automatically.
