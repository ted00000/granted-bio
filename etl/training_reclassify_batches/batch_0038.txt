phr: PROJECT NARRATIVE Increasing the diversity of the model systems we study will proportionally increase our understanding of how sensory systems evolve, reveal conserved principles of sensory processing, and ultimately help us better extrapolate results we obtain in animal studies to the human nervous system, in both normal and disease states. Here, we propose to expand our understanding of the organization of temperature and humidity sensing circuits by introducing the desert fly Drosophila mojavensis (adapted to dry and heat conditions) as a new model alongside the common laboratory fruit fly Drosophila melanogaster.
