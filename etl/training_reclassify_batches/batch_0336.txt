phr: PROJECT NARRATIVE
Malaria is the leading parasitic threat to humans and a major infectious cause of morbidity and mortality. While
there have been concerted efforts to address vector-borne transmission, blood transfusion remains a
neglected mode of transmission and strategies to contend with transfusion-transmitted malaria (TTM) are the
exception. We propose to conduct one of the largest studies of TTM to date, to characterize the epidemiology
of malaria in blood donors, as well as the risk of infection to transfusion recipients in a malaria-endemic
country. We will also evaluate the economic viability and relative performance of different testing modalities to
develop evidence-based strategies to contend with TTM in endemic and non-endemic countries alike.

studies, will be invaluable to furthering the clinical understanding of OSA.
