Organ structures, vital for function, are sculpted inside developing embryos within specific geometric contexts,

title: Diet and metabolic factors in the pathogenesis of microscopic colitis
org_name: MASSACHUSETTS GENERAL HOSPITAL
phr: PROJECT NARRATIVE
Microscopic colitis, a relapsing inflammatory disease of the large intestine caused by a dysregulated immune
response to changes in the gut luminal microenvironment, affects approximately 1 in 115 females and 1 in 286
males over a lifetime but has no FDA-approved therapies. Diet is the largest contributor to the gut luminal
microenvironment but remains unexplored in microscopic colitis; as such, this proposal comprehensively
examines the role of dietary patterns and downstream plasma and gut-microbiome-derived metabolites in
disease pathogenesis and treatment outcomes. This proposal represents a critical step to advancing our
understanding of microscopic colitis pathogenesis, with the goal of ultimately advancing treatment strategies for
this debilitating disease.

title: Investigating the Role of GABAergic Interneurons in the Dorsomedial Striatum in Goal-Directed Behavior
org_name: UNIVERSITY OF PENNSYLVANIA
phr: Project Narrative
Goal-directed behaviors maximize rewards in changing environments, and abnormalities in goal-directed
behavior are a functionally impairing feature of a range of neuropsychiatric diseases. Accumulating evidence
suggests a role for dorsomedial striatal GABAergic parvalbumin-positive fast-spiking interneurons (PV+ FSIs)
and somatostatin-positive low-threshold spiking interneurons (SST+ LTSIs) in value-based goal-directed
behavior; however, this has not been directly tested. I aim to record and causally manipulate PV+ FSIs and SST+
LTSIs in mice performing a value-based goal-directed behavioral task to elucidate the role of striatal interneurons
in striatal function and goal-directed behavior.
