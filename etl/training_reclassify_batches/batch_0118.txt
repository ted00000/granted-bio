title: Examining Potential Mobile Measures of Strain, or the Accumulated Effects of Exposure to Demands
org_name: UNIVERSITY OF SOUTHERN CALIFORNIA
phr: PROJECT NARRATIVE In the recovery from work literature, strain refers to effects (e.g., on affect, physiology, fatigue levels, etc.) of exposure to stressors or any other demands which, if allowed to accumulate, can lead to impaired health and performance. The goals of this study are to use digital health data to see if physiological measures, such as heart rate variability, and performance-based measures, such as mobile cognitive testing, can serve as indicators of accrued strain, which is often unobservable and hence easy to ignore. The ability to utilize real- world digital health data for the identification of strain will enable enhancement of existing, and creation of new, worker recovery interventions, which could prevent the negative effects of strain accumulation.
