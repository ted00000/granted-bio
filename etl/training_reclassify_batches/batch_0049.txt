title: Dissecting cholinergic modulation of interneurons underlying state-dependent processing in mouse visual cortex
org_name: DUKE UNIVERSITY
phr: Project Narrative
Viewing the same scene or object can drive different responses in visual cortex depending on whether the
observer is stationary or moving, engaged or passive. Enabling this context-dependent processing is thought to
be a key function of the neuromodulator acetylcholine, and deficits of acetylcholine are associated with disorders
ranging from Alzheimer's Disease to schizophrenia; however, the mechanisms through which acetylcholine
modulates the cortex are incompletely understood, hampering the development of treatments. This proposal
dissects the action of acetylcholine on specific cell types in the visual cortex, clarifying how this neuromodulator
impacts components of the cortical circuity to drive context-specific processing.
