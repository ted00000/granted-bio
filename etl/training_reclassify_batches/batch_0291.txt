application_id: 11165831
activity_code: F32
title: Characterizing representational geometry and learning across primate visual streams
org_name: COLUMBIA UNIVERSITY HEALTH SCIENCES
phr: Project Narrative
The ventral stream of the visual system has long been known to contribute to aphasia or memory loss when
damaged, however it remains unclear how specific parts of the ventral stream contribute to these deficits. The
current study will separately characterize responses in the ventral stream, and computationally model different
sub-parts of the ventral stream, including the perirhinal (Pr) and parhippocamapal (Ph) subdivisions.
Understanding the ventral visual stream at this level of granularity will allow more precise predictions of how
damage to different parts of the brain might affect behavior, and therefore open avenues for the development of
novel diagnostic tools for localizing the locus of a lesion neurologically.

org_name: EMORY UNIVERSITY

Child obesity and metabolic dysfunction-associated steatotic liver disease (MASLD) are driven by
the early onset and progression of insulin resistance, fueled by diets high in sugars. Over 7 million
children in the United States are estimated to be affected by MASLD and the accompanying
features of the cardiometabolic syndrome including abdominal obesity, insulin resistance, and
dyslipidemia. The aims of this K24 will expand the mentorship of new patient-oriented
researchers and increase patient-oriented research in the area of prevention of childhood
cardiometabolic disease.
