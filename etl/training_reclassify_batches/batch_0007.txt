title: Defining the transition from deep quiescence to senescence
org_name: UNIVERSITY OF COLORADO
phr: PROJECT NARRATIVE
Quiescent cancer cells are a source of drug resistance as they evade chemotherapeutic treatments targeted at
cycling cells and retain their ability to proliferate in the future, whereas senescent cancer cells are tumor-
suppressive as they limit the proliferation of damaged cells and recruit the immune system. The proposed
research will investigate how cells transit between quiescence and senescence and will determine the
mechanism by which quiescent cells permanently lose their ability to proliferate, thus becoming senescent.
Quiescent and senescent cells arise side-by-side following chemotherapeutic treatment, thus, a mechanistic
understanding of how cells choose between these fates would aid in the development of more targeted and
effective chemotherapy treatments.
