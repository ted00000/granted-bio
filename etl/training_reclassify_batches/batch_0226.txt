title: Elucidating the role of hepatic mTORC2 as a key regulator of carbohydrate metabolism in non-alcoholic fatty liver disease
org_name: UNIV OF MASSACHUSETTS MED SCH WORCESTER
phr: Project Narrative Non-alcoholic fatty liver disease (NAFLD) is an increasingly prevalent condition effecting about 30% of Americans and costing the U.S about $103 billion per year, with no FDA approved treatments beyond lifestyle alterations. Mammalian target of rapamycin complex 2 (mTORC2) is emerging as a major metabolic hub and its activation has been linked to NAFLD in mice due to its role in regulating lipid metabolism in response to high fat diets, however, the major dietary supplements attributed to NAFLD prevalence are carbohydrates, especially fructose. In this proposal, I will investigate the role of hepatic mTORC2 in regulating carbohydrate derived acetyl-CoA production and utilization in NAFLD, using a combination of molecular biology, metabolomic, and proteomic approaches.
