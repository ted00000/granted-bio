phr: Project Narrative Adolescent and adult patients with repaired Tetralogy of Fallot, who are at increased risk of heart failure and sudden cardiac death, require frequent cardiac MRI evaluation of right ventricular volumes (which are poorly assessed by traditional ECG and echocardiography). To improve risk stratification and reduce the need for cardiac MRI, which is resource-intensive and unavailable to some, we will create an algorithm to predict right ventricular size, function, and clinical outcome from ECG waveforms, echocardiographic video, and clinical data using deep learning technology.
