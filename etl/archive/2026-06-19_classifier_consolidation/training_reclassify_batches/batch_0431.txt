title: Role of Extracellular Long-Chain Acyl-carnitines in the Normal and Stressed Heart
org_name: JOHNS HOPKINS UNIVERSITY
phr: PROJECT NARRATIVE
Recently, we have demonstrated that patients with heart failure have decreased acyl-carnitines in heart biopsies
as compared to controls, but still have normal (HFpEF) or increased (HFrEF) acyl-carnitines in the blood. This
project tests the hypothesis that extracellular acyl-carnitines are taken up and oxidized by cardiomyocytes and
intact hearts, that this rises under hemodynamic or metabolic stress, and is impaired by reduced transporters in
HF. Understanding how extracellular acyl-carnitines are (or are not) utilized in the normal heart, and hearts under
acute or chronic stress could lead to better strategies to metabolically counter heart failure.
