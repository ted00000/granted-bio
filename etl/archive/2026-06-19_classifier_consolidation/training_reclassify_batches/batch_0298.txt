lifespan.

title: The Role of the Gut Microbiome in Post-Sepsis Skeletal Muscle Atrophy
org_name: UNIVERSITY OF IOWA
phr: PROJECT NARRATIVE
Sepsis is a common and often deadly condition of organ dysfunction due to infection that is responsible for 1.7
million hospitalizations and an estimated 260,000 deaths annually in the United States; survivors of sepsis are
often left debilitated due to muscle atrophy, which hinders independence and reduces quality of life.
Unfortunately, other than physical rehabilitation, there are no treatments currently available for post-sepsis
muscle atrophy because we do not understand how sepsis causes muscle atrophy. This project will study how
the gut microbiome, the bacteria that live in the human intestinal tract, contributes to muscle atrophy during
sepsis, with a goal of identifying new targets for treatment.
