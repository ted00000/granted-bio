title: Discovery and pathogenic characterization of novel monogenic causes of bladder dysfunction.
org_name: BOSTON CHILDREN'S HOSPITAL
phr: NARRATIVE Congenital anomalies of the kidney and urinary tract (CAKUT) are the most common cause of chronic kidney disease in the first three decades of life. However, therapeutic interventions remain limited, and many children progress to end-stage renal disease. Recent studies have revealed over 40 novel monogenic causes of human CAKUT, the majority of which coalesce into pathways that regulate nephrogenesis. Monogenic causes of lower urinary tract disease are largely unknown. The aim of this career development grant is to discover novel monogenic causes of bladder dysfunction and to explore the molecular mechanisms by which mutations in these genes cause human disease.
