org_name: UNIVERSITY OF CALIFORNIA LOS ANGELES
phr: PROJECT NARRATIVE
Addiction is a major determinant of mortality and societal burden. Despite high public health significance of the
problem, treatment and prevention of drug addiction are only modestly effective. The UCLA Training Program in
the Translational Neuroscience of Drug Addiction (TNDA) provides multidisciplinary training that spans the
fundamental bases of addiction from the genome to neural phenotypes and ultimately the syndrome of addiction,
with particular strength in molecular and cellular, behavioral and systems, and cognitive and clinical
neuroscience.

The purpose of this training grant renewal, Training Program in Population Studies, is to provide exceptional
demographic training to the next generation of social and behavioral scientists. Through mentoring,
coursework, and professional socialization, our predoctoral and postdoctoral fellows will learn the necessary
skills to identify, analyze, and produce scholarship critical to addressing society’s pressing social and public
health issues. Our predoctoral and postdoctoral fellows will benefit from the innovative and high-quality
scholarship and expertise of our training faculty and they will learn to conduct research with the highest ethical
research standards, scientific rigor, and reproducibility.
