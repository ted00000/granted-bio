title: The role of retinoic acid signaling in patterning the human cerebral cortex
org_name: UNIVERSITY OF CALIFORNIA, SAN FRANCISCO
phr: PROJECT NARRATIVE
 Different areas of the brain are composed of unique cell types and connectivity patterns that subserve their
unique functional roles, and disruption of areal patterning during development can lead to neurologic and
neuropsychiatric disorders such as autism and schizophrenia. Recent studies have implicated a role for
retinoic acid, derived from Vitamin A, in patterning of the cerebral cortex, and this proposal will explore the
mechanism by which this areal specification occurs. These experiments will generate foundational knowledge
about the mechanisms of areal specification in the brain, and may help explain genetic susceptibility to
neurodevelopmental disorders associated with abnormal arealization.
