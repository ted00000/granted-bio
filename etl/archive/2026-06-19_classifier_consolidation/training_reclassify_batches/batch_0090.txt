title: Transporting treatment effects from clinical trials to real-world populations with co-occurring opioid and stimulant use disorders
org_name: OREGON HEALTH & SCIENCE UNIVERSITY
phr: PROJECT NARRATIVE
Treatment options for people with co-occurring opioid and stimulant use disorders are limited, partially because
this group is inadequately represented in treatment efficacy evaluations. The proposed research uses a novel
data-fusion approach to translate narrowly-focused clinical trial results to broader, representative populations
with co-occurring opioid and stimulant use disorders. This study will demonstrate the effectiveness of
treatments for real-world patients with these co-occurring substance use disorders; clinicians, researchers,
policymakers, and other stakeholders may use results to select treatments that improve the health of people
using more than one substance.

title: Novel Pathways and Therapeutic Targets for Cisplatin-Associated Acute Kidney Injury
org_name: BRIGHAM AND WOMEN'S HOSPITAL
phr: PROJECT NARRATIVE Acute kidney injury (AKI) is a common complication of cisplatin chemotherapy, and effective therapeutic and preventative strategies for cisplatin-associated AKI (CP-AKI) are lacking. In this proposal, we will study the association between intravenous magnesium (IV Mg), mannitol, metformin, and statins and CP-AKI using a target trial framework; we will evaluate in a double-blind, randomized, placebo-controlled trial whether the administration of IV Mg reduces the risk of CP-AKI in patients receiving hyperthermic intraoperative chemotherapy with cisplatin; and we will examine in a prospective cohort study whether urinary quinolinate normalized to tryptophan, a novel marker and potential therapeutic target for AKI in other contexts, is elevated in patients with CP-AKI.
