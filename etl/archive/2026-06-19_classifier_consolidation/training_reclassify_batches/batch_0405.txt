org_name: BRIGHAM AND WOMEN'S HOSPITAL
phr: PROJECT NARRATIVE
Although the field of rheumatology is beginning to subtype rheumatoid arthritis patients using relative
abundances of certain cell types in the synovium, our understanding of what these cell types do and how they
work together is limited. In this proposal, we will define how immunity is spatially organized in the rheumatoid
arthritis synovium, both across rheumatoid arthritis subtypes and in comparison to other autoimmune diseases,
using cutting-edge techniques from statistics and artificial intelligence. This will enable mechanistic
understanding of known disease subtypes, discovery of improved disease subtypes, and extrapolation of
mechanistic and therapeutic hypotheses from other related diseases.
