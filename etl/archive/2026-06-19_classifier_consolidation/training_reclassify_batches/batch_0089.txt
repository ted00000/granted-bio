phr: Public Health Relevance Statement
Smell dysfunction and its neurologic consequences following viral infection remain poorly understood despite a
growing prevalence following the current SARS-CoV-2 pandemic. Studying epidemiologic risk factors and the
associations between smell loss and deficits in central neurologic processing present an opportunity to better
characterize post-viral smell dysfunction, identify at-risk populations for preventative interventions, and inform
future investigation into central brain circuitry processing of scents.
