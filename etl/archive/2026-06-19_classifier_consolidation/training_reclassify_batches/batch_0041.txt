Despite the emerging link between air pollution exposure – specifically PM2.5 – and heighted risk of adolescent
anxiety, the neurobehavioral mechanisms underlying this association are unknown. The proposed F32 project
will be the first to evaluate whether exposure to recent PM2.5 concentrations is associated with poorer extinction
recall, lower frontolimbic activation, and higher anxiety symptoms during adolescence, a period of frontolimbic

interventions to stem the etiology of anxiety in at-risk pollution-exposed youth.
