title: The Evolution of Sexually Dimorphic Morphogenesis
org_name: NEW YORK UNIVERSITY
phr: PROJECT NARRATIVE
Morphogenesis is a universally important process that is controlled by precise
spatiotemporal regulation of gene transcription (expression). Advances have been made
in elucidating the cell biological machinery of morphogenesis; however, we lack an understanding of
how transcriptional regulation controls this machinery, why this control is robust, and what parts of
this control are conserved vs. plastic. This study will inform us about the architecture of transcriptional
control of morphogenesis and how it has evolved, thus providing a basis to identify key conserved
