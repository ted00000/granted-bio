org_name: MBARARA UNIVERSITY/SCIENCE/ TECHNOLOGY
phr: PROJECT NARRATIVE
People with HIV (PWH) are at increased risk of developing coronary artery disease (CAD) due to an
accelerated inflammatory process resulting in formation of non-calcified plaques, not easily detected by
standardized diagnostic methods. Pericoronary adipose tissue (PCAT) may serve as a feasible alternative
imaging technique for early detection of coronary inflammation and CAD in people with HIV (PWH). This
project will leverage data from two established cohorts to determine relationships between HIV-related
inflammation, PCAT, and subclinical CAD among PWH in Uganda.
