phr: PROJECT NARRATIVE Insulin resistance and its associated diseases, including type 2 diabetes (T2D), obesity, and metabolic dysfunction-associated fatty liver disease (MAFLD), are some of the leading causes of morbidity and death worldwide and our understanding of what drives insulin resistance is limited. We recently discovered that the insulin receptor forms dynamic, liquid-like biomolecular condensates, which become dysfunctional in insulin resistance, becoming less insulin responsive, less dynamic, and less functionally active. This project seeks to understand how insulin receptor condensates are formed and become altered in insulin resistance.

title: Lysosome homeostasis in aging-related diseases
org_name: WEILL MEDICAL COLL OF CORNELL UNIV
phr: Project Narrative
Lysosomes become dysfunctional during aging and this is linked to the development of numerous diseases
including many adult-onset neurodegenerative conditions. My studies are uncovering how lysosome function
can be restored through mechanisms that remove dysfunctional lysosomes and generate new ones. This
research is critical to understanding the origins of aging-related diseases and to uncovering new therapeutic
opportunities.
