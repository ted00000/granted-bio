title: Epigenetic Regulation of Microglia Dopamine System Interactions
org_name: DUKE UNIVERSITY
phr: PROJECT NARRATIVE
The development of the dopamine system is a tightly regulated process that has great implications on social and
reward associated behaviors. Microglia, the resident immune cells of the brain, selectively engulf (phagocytose)
dopamine receptors during adolescent development, however, what regulates this microglia function remains
unknown. I will investigate the role of dopaminergic signaling in regulating microglial chromatin remodeling and
phagocytic function through adolescence impacting social behavior—providing necessary foundational
knowledge on microglial development in a key reward associated brain region.

activity_code: K08
title: Facilitation of Information Exchange for Shared Decision Making for Lung Cancer Screening
org_name: UNIV OF MASSACHUSETTS MED SCH WORCESTER
phr: PROJECT NARRATIVE
Shared decision making prior to lung cancer screening is recommended since lung cancer screening has both
benefits and risks. However, shared decision making rarely happens in primary care settings due to difficulty in
identifying lung cancer screening eligible patients, lack of patient awareness, lack of provider knowledge, and
limited visit time. This pilot study will compare a multi-strategy technology-assisted implementation program to
usual care on lung cancer screening uptake, and will provide a foundation for a future large-scale pragmatic
trial to improve the uptake of and longitudinal adherence to annual lung cancer screening by supporting shared
decision making.
