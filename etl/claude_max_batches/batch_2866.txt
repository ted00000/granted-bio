phr: Project Narrative Chronic musculoskeletal pain in older age is common and often leads to lower physical activity in this population; however, some older adults with pain are physically active. This proposal investigates the neural and behavioral measures of motivation that may protect against the detrimental effects of pain on physical activity. The results of this study may help identify modifiable protective factors and inform future targets to promote pain resiliency in older adults.

title: Impact of brain estrogens on cognition and brain aging in a non-human primate
org_name: UNIVERSITY OF MASSACHUSETTS AMHERST
phr: NARRATIVE
Breast cancer patients using aromatase inhibitors (AIs) as an adjuvant therapy often report severe side effects,
including hot flashes, cognitive impairment, insomnia and mood changes. Despite long-term use in humans,
little is known about the long-term effects of AIs on brain function. This proposal uses a primate model to
understand how AIs affect the brain and behavior and to test whether a drug that delivers estrogens
exclusively to the brain can safely alleviate these deficits.
