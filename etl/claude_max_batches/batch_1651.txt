org_name: RESEARCH INST NATIONWIDE CHILDREN'S HOSP
phr: Project Narrative
Prune Belly Syndrome (PBS) is a severe human urologic disorder with poorly understood
genetic causes. DNA mutations in the X-linked gene filamin A (FLNA) identified in PBS cases
will be tested to find their dysfunctional effects critically altering bladder detrusor smooth muscle
development and disrupting binding to distinct partners, to guide future treatment strategies.

Current treatment options for xerostomia are inadequate, highlighting an urgent need to better understand the
drivers of the underlying diseases and develop new therapeutic approaches. This project will test the
hypothesis that targeting Type 4 cAMP-phosphodiesterases (PDE4s) can limit local inflammation as a main
driver of salivary gland hypo-/dysfunction, and potentiate both adrenergic- and muscarinic salivary secretions,
thus alleviating the principal symptom in xerostomia.
