title: Mechanisms of sodium channel clustering at the neuromuscular junction
org_name: BAYLOR COLLEGE OF MEDICINE
phr: Project Narrative
Na+ channels are clustered at the neuromuscular junction (NMJ) and in a perijunctional
zone where they function to prevent synapse fatigue. However, the mechanisms
responsible for NMJ Na+ channel clustering and assembly of the perijunctional zone are
unknown. Here, we will determine the mechanisms responsible and investigate how
these change with aging and disease.
