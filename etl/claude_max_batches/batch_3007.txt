Oral Squamous Cell Carcinoma (OSCC) is a common and complex disease with limited therapeutic options. We
propose to investigate how cancer cells communicate with other supporting cells in the OSCC tumor
microenvironment to facilitate the progression of this disease. Such studies are essential for understanding the
mechanism(s) by which cancer cells grow and spread, and for identification of candidate biomarkers and
therapeutic targets to better diagnose and treat OSCC.
