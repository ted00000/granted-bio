phr: The role of functionally distinct hepatic T cell subsets in providing protective immunity from hepatotropic viruses, such as hepatitis C virus (HCV), or mediating liver pathology during inflammatory liver disease is poorly defined. Using a mouse model of an HCV-related virus we will investigate the functional properties and potential interplay of distinct type 1 and type 2 T cell and natural killer T (NKT) cell subsets during different stages of hepacivirus infection in vivo.
