title: Mechanisms of immune-epithelial crosstalk in tissue repair
org_name: ICAHN SCHOOL OF MEDICINE AT MOUNT SINAI
phr: PROJECT NARRATIVE Immunosuppression is a major risk factor for non-healing wounds, indicating that signals derived from immune cells are necessary for optimal repair. The goal of this proposal is to understand how type 17 immune cells are activated in response to injury and elucidate their mechanistic crosstalk with the epithelium.

title: Live-attenuated Rift Valley fever vaccines:  comparative mechanisms of trans-placental transmission and vaccine efficacy for developing fetuses
org_name: UNIVERSITY OF PITTSBURGH AT PITTSBURGH
phr: PROJECT NARRATIVE
Rift Valley fever (RVF) is a mosquito-borne disease that is known for causing fetal death in
livestock animals; indeed, live-attenuated RVFV veterinary vaccines can still cause fetal infection
in pregnant animals. Using a pregnant rat model, this proposal will determine how current and
next-generation vaccines cross the maternal-fetal boundary and the correlates of immune
protection required to prevent fetal infection.
